How We'll Actually Use AI in Apps

One assistant to rule them all. Not fifty different chatbots.

Jiuhe Wang  |  Mar 08, 2026

The Mess We're In

Here's how software has worked for decades: you open an app, you do a thing. Excel for spreadsheets. Photoshop for images. Slack for messages. Each app was its own island.

Then AI happened. Now every app has its own chatbot:

Photoshop AI
Notion AI
Word AI
Excel AI
Slack AI
Each works differently. None talk to each other. You're the integration layer, copy-pasting between apps like it's 1999.

I think there's a better way. One assistant. Many capabilities. The apps just do what they're good at.

Two Ways This Could Go

Where we are
Fragmented AI

Every app builds its own AI. Different personalities. Different limitations. No shared context.

  • Photoshop: "Generative Fill"
  • Notion: "Notion AI"
  • Excel: "Copilot"
  • Slack: "Slack AI"
Result: Context switching hell
Where we're headed
Unified Assistant

One assistant orchestrates everything. Apps expose capabilities. Assistant figures out the how.

  • One context across all apps
  • Apps focus on capabilities, not chat
  • Assistant routes requests
  • Natural handoffs between tools
Result: One conversation, any tools

The future isn't fifty AI chatbots in fifty apps. It's one assistant that uses fifty capabilities.

Apps Become Capabilities

In the unified model, apps stop being experiences and start being capabilities. Spotify might expose:

Morning run request
You
Going for a 30-minute run. Play my workout playlist but swap slow songs for 90s bangers.
Assistant
Checking your workout playlist...
Spotify
Workout playlist: 15 songs. 3 have BPM < 120.
Assistant
Found 90s alternatives. Creating "Morning Run - Mar 8" playlist...
Health App
Workout started. Good luck!
Assistant
Done! Playing now. Want me to check your pace at the 15-minute mark?

You talked. The assistant orchestrated Spotify, found songs, created a playlist, and started your workout tracking. You never opened either app.

Enterprise: Integration Hell

If consumer is messy, enterprise is a disaster. A typical company uses:

Salesforce
Workday
SAP
Jira
47 more
These systems were bought in different years by different people with different consultants. Some "integrations" are literally daily CSV files emailed between departments.

Where AI Actually Helps

Traditional integration: mapping fields, writing transformation code, testing, deploying, praying it doesn't break. AI makes it better by understanding what you're trying to do, not just executing commands.

Data Silos

Customer data in Salesforce. Employees in Workday. Money in SAP. None connect.

Auth Nightmare

SAML here, OAuth there, LDAP somewhere else. Plus that spreadsheet of passwords.

API Zoo

REST, SOAP, GraphQL, and custom XML-RPC from a Java app that predates Y2K.

Drifting Semantics

"Revenue" means one thing in Salesforce, another in SAP, another in the CFO's Excel.

AI doesn't make enterprise integration easy—it makes it possible without armies of consultants.

Consumer vs Enterprise

Factor Consumer Enterprise
Complexity Dozens of apps Hundreds of systems
Standards Emerging (Siri, Google) None (vendor lock-in)
Integration Vendor handles it Custom per company
AI's value Convenience Making the impossible possible
Time to value Immediate Requires setup
Both converge on the same pattern: one intelligence layer that orchestrates everything. Consumer assistants come from Apple/Google. Enterprise assistants get built per organization.

My Guess at the Timeline

Now (March 2026)
Fragmented Present

Every app has its own AI. ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot—they're islands. You copy-paste between them. Enterprise integration remains expensive and painful.

2026-2027 (Next 12-18 months)
Orchestration Begins

Siri, Google Assistant deeply integrate with third-party apps. MCP protocol gains rapid adoption. Enterprise AI tools make integrations 10x faster. Early adopters start showing real results.

2027-2028
Standards Emerge

Apps expose "AI-ready" interfaces as standard. Unified assistant pattern becomes normal. Enterprise platforms go mainstream. Late adopters scramble to catch up.

2028+
Ambient Intelligence

"Opening an app" feels quaint. You state intent, assistant handles it. App boundaries dissolve. Enterprise fully AI-orchestrated. The old way feels ancient.

My Take

We're in the awkward phase. Every app is building its own AI, creating chatbots that don't talk to each other. It's not sustainable.

By 2028, "opening an app" will feel like "booting your computer" feels today—possible, but why would you? You'll just talk to your assistant.

The winners will be:

  • Apps that are excellent capabilities without pretending to chat
  • Assistants that become trusted orchestrators of our digital lives
Apps will become like muscles—specialized capabilities that the brain (your assistant) uses without you thinking about the biology.
